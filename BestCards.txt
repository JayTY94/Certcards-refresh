Bandwidth vs. Throughput
- Bandwidth: The maximum rate of data transfer across a network path, typically measured in Mbps or Gbps. It represents the network's capacity.
- Throughput: The actual rate of successful data transfer achieved, often lower than the bandwidth due to factors like network congestion and protocol overhead.

Fileless Malware
A type of malware that operates entirely in memory rather than writing files to disk. Because it does not create executable files on storage drives, it can evade traditional signature-based antivirus tools. Execution typically occurs through legitimate processes, scripting engines, or memory injection, with all malicious activity occurring in RAM.

802.11 Management Frames
Frames used in Wi-Fi networks to support discovery, association, authentication, and disassociation between clients and access points. Early wireless standards did not protect management frames, meaning they were sent in clear text without authentication or validation, which enabled attacks such as deauthentication and spoofing.
